# Project charter

## Purpose

Reduce repeated SHACL design effort by publishing documented, testable validation patterns whose reuse assumptions and limitations are explicit.

## Scope

The project explores a catalogue model; reusable SHACL Core and clearly tiered extension modules; abstract model roles and local mappings; deterministic specialisation and assembly; executable examples and expected reports; portability, security, performance and compatibility evidence; multilingual documentation and messages; provenance, maturity, versioning and deprecation; and publication-ready metadata including a ValPub view.

## Non-goals

The project will not define a universal domain model, infer semantic equivalence from OWL alone, promise identical reports from all engines, replace application-specific modelling review, certify compliance or data truth, require direct imports, select a permanent namespace or governing institution during exploration, or accumulate patterns merely to increase catalogue size.

## Success criteria

Reuse is successful when a competent shape author can determine applicability, specialise a pattern without hidden semantic changes, reproduce its tests, understand engine and threat limitations, and upgrade or remain pinned deliberately. If copying remains clearer or safer, the catalogue has failed its purpose.

## Principles

Evidence before maturity; explicit assumptions; least-powerful portable feature set; deterministic generation; versioned semantics; human-reviewable output; no implicit targets; conservative equivalence; accessible multilingual metadata; and honest negative claims.
